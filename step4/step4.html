4.Left ventricular volume measurement

The reason why we are finding the left ventricular volume is to decide which stage out of the 10 cardiac cycle phases is end systole and end diastole. The cardiac cycle phase with lowest ventricular volume is automatically marked as ES and the most as ED. Although the ventricular volume is predefined, we can always add a new slice or clear the selected slice to get the most precise ventricular volume.

Under the Workflow Assistant, choose "Assessment," then scroll down to "Volume Measurement" and select "Ventricle."

The segmented views of the left ventricle will be displayed. Scroll through each slice to view and adjust. The ventricular outline is generally defined with green color lines, but you can add or clear slices to calculate an accurate volume for each stage of the cardiac cycle.

Under Workflow Assistant, choose "ED/ES." You will see the cardiac cycle bar with a blue color flow, which changes according to the volume specified for each cardiac cycle phase.

The cardiac cycle phase with the lowest ventricular volume is automatically marked as End Systole (ES), and the phase with the highest volume is marked as End Diastole (ED).

3. Locating Trigones
Locating the trigones is important because we will use them as reference points for building a plane in the later steps. Additionally, these two trigone points serve as the base for forming the D-shape of the annulus when editing its shape.

Locate the annulus until the aortic cusps are no longer visible from your view. Click here to see what it means by the visible aortic cusp.


Identify the trigones, which are marked with pink and green dots.


Align the pink and green dots with the blue cursor line.


Rotate the blue cursor line in the long axis view (top right side) to align the green and pink dots. Each time they fall into a vertical line, shift each point to the blue cursor line (anteroposterior AP view) in the short axis view (left side).


Note: The CT scans imported must be of complete cardiac cycle. Click here to see what complete cardiac cycle is.

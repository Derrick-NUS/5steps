2. Finding Mitral Annulus

Before proceeding with any further steps, it is crucial to accurately identify the location of the mitral annulus ring. Variations in the position of the annulus ring will significantly impact subsequent measurements, including the distance and ventricular volume of the left ventricle. These measurements are essential for assessing the end systole and diastole stages. Therefore, precise detection of the mitral annulus ring is essential for accurate assessment and diagnosis.

First, click on “Find Mitral Annulus”.

When the page appears, move at least one point and adjust the points to match the annulus circumference from the top view.

You can choose to adjust the points individually by selecting “Single Point” or visualize and adjust all points at once by selecting “Multi Point” on the right side.

This process provides a rough outline of the annulus circumference. To achieve a more precise view, you will still need to locate the trigones.
